Dramatis Personae

  • The Bezúkhovs

    • Count Cyril Vladímirovich Bezúkhov, a wealthy old grandee of the Empress Catherine’s time. At his death his illegimate son Pierre inherits his title and estates.

    • Monsieur Pierre, afterwards Count Peter Kirílych Bezúkhov. The Count’s illegitimate son, educated abroad, and easily led both into dissipation and into idealistic theories of life. He marries first the Princess Hélène, and afterwards the Countess Natasha Rostóva.

  • The Bolkónskis

    • Prince Nicholas Andréevitch Bolkónski. A harsh martinet, full of old-time prejudices, living a bitter, lonely life at his estate of Bald Hills; father of Prince Andrew and Princess Mariya.

    • Prince Andrew Nikoláevich Bolkónski. Also called André and Andreyusha. Adjutant or aide to General Kutúzov; wounded at Austerlitz; proprietor of Bugacharovo; engaged to Countess Natasha Rostóva.

    • Princess Elizabeth Karlovna Bolkónskaya (née Mienen). Known as Lise; Prince Andrew’s wife, who dies giving birth their son.

    • Prince Nicholas Andréevitch Bolkónski; also known as Koko. Prince Andrew’s baby son.

    • Princess Mary Nikoláevna Bolkónskaya; also known as Marie. Marries Nicholas Rostóv and becomes the Countess Rostóva.

  • The Drubetskóys

    • Prince Borís Drubetskóy. A relation of the Rostóvs; he is cold, calculating, and selfish, and through influence is rapidly advanced.

    • Julie Drubetskáya, née Karágina. Borís’ wife.

    • Princess Anna Mikháylovna Drubetskáya. Poor but intriguing; Borís’ mother.

  • The Kurágins

    • Prince Vasíli Sergéevich Kurágin.

    • Prince Hippolyte Kurágin. Prince Vasíli’s older son; in the diplomatic service, but dissipated and foolish.

    • Prince Anatole Kurágin. Prince Vasíli’s younger son; a spendthrift who aspires to Princess Mary Nikoláevna’s hand, but proves himself unworthy.

    • Princess Hélène Kurágina; also known as Ellen. Prince Vasíli’s daughter, afterwards the unfaithful first wife of Pierre Bezúkhov.

  • The Rostóvs

    • Count Ilyá Andréevich Rostóv. A wealthy but extravagant proprietor whose affairs go from bad to worse.

    • Countess Nataly Rostóva (née Shinshiná), his wife.

    • Count Nicholas Rostóv. Count Rostóv’s son, open-hearted, gallant, generous; serving in the cavalry; at first engaged to his cousin Sonya, afterwards married to the Princess Mary Bolkónskaya.

    • Count Peter Rostóv; also known as Pétya. Count Rostóv’s son.

    • Countess Véra Rostóva. Count Rostóv’s oldest daughter; later married to Alphonse Berg.

    • Countess Nataly Rostóva; also known as Natásha. Count Rostóv’s youngest daughter; engaged to Prince Andrew Bolkónski, but later married to Count Pierre Bezukhóv.

    • Sónya (no last name mentioned). Count Rostóv’s niece; engaged for a time to Count Nicholas.

  • Others

    • Joseph Alexéevich Bazdéev. The Freemason, Pierre’s “Benefactor.”

    • Alphonse Kárlovich Berg. A conceited young officer who “gets on” and marries Countess Véra Rostóva.

    • Vasalí Feodorovitch Denisóv. Also known as Váska; gallant soldier and poet, in love with Natásha Rostóva.

    • Dólokhov (no first name mentioned). A gambler and roué, who offers himself to Sónya but is rejected; brave but bad.

    • Márya Ivánovna Dólokhova. The fond mother of Dólokhov.

    • Anna Pávlovna Schérer. Maid-of-honour and favorite of the current empress.
